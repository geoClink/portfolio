About Me

My Story

I’m a developer passionate about making learning fun and accessible through "edu-tainment"—educational entertainment that engages and inspires.

I graduated from Colorado State University and am currently at the Apple Developer Academy, where I focus on building products that are useful, inclusive, and technically strong. My transition into software was driven by curiosity and a hands-on approach to solving real problems. I care deeply about UI/UX and creating experiences that feel intuitive and accessible for everyone.

My recent work spans travel discovery, game systems, civic technology, and educational tools. Through projects like Stamped!, TakeFlight, CommonSight, and LearnToCode, I’ve learned how to translate ideas into polished user flows, production-ready features, and clear product narratives. I coded this portfolio myself as my first HTML, CSS, and JavaScript project, and later created LearnToCode as my second web project, expanding my skills in multi-language educational tools.

Outside of work, I love to ride my bike, explore new restaurants, and volunteer at local food banks. These experiences keep me inspired and connected to my community, and they shape how I approach building products—with empathy, creativity, and a focus on real-world impact.

Currently, I’m contributing through an internship, helping a small business owner shape an investor-ready demo. That experience has sharpened how I balance speed, clarity, and user trust in every build.

What began as a curiosity has evolved into a dedicated pursuit of excellence. As I prepare to graduate the Apple Developer Academy in Detroit, MI, I’m proud of the foundations I’ve laid, but even more motivated by the challenges ahead. What defines my approach isn't just the code—it’s the intention behind it. I thrive on understanding the 'why' before building the 'how,' ensuring every solution I create serves a clear, human purpose.

What I Build

I build SwiftUI-first experiences with an emphasis on accessibility, interaction design, and maintainable architecture. I care about products that feel intuitive on day one and scale with real user needs.

  • SwiftUI app development with Firebase, SpriteKit, and persistent data workflows
  • Cross-platform UX decisions for iPad and macOS, including full keyboard compatibility
  • Accessibility-minded product decisions and interaction patterns from the start

What Guides Me

My approach is simple: understand the user context, build with intention, and ship work that is clear to both users and stakeholders. Accessibility, clarity, and thoughtful details are not extras; they are the work.

I believe strong products start with user empathy and are sustained by clean, reliable implementation.

I believe design should reduce friction, clarify decisions, and make complex systems feel simple.

I believe accessibility should be integrated from the first prototype through final iteration.

I believe great software respects platform strengths and meets people where they are across devices.
