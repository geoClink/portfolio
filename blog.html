Career Experience | March 2026

My Journey at Apple Developer Academy Detroit

Attending the Apple Developer Academy in Detroit has been a transformative experience. I’ve worked alongside talented peers, learned from industry mentors, and built real-world apps from concept to launch. The Academy’s hands-on approach pushed me to grow as a developer, designer, and collaborator. I gained deep skills in Swift, UI/UX, accessibility, and teamwork—plus exposure to Apple’s latest tools and frameworks. The Detroit community is vibrant and supportive, and the Academy’s focus on inclusivity and innovation inspired me to build products that make a difference. If you’re considering the Academy, expect to be challenged, supported, and empowered to create your best work.

Tools & IDE | March 2026

My Thoughts on Xcode 26.3

Xcode 26.3 feels like a mature, polished IDE for Apple developers. The new features—especially improved SwiftUI previews, faster build times, and enhanced accessibility tools—make daily development smoother and more productive. I appreciate the stability and performance improvements, as well as the expanded support for Apple Intelligence APIs. The UI is cleaner, and the debugging experience is more intuitive. One standout is how Xcode is starting to support agentic coding: workflows where AI and automation help generate, refactor, and test code alongside the developer. This shift makes coding more collaborative and efficient, letting me focus on creative problem-solving while routine tasks are handled by smart tools. While there are still areas for improvement (like simulator speed and occasional SwiftUI quirks), Xcode 26.3 is a strong step forward. It’s a tool I rely on every day, and it keeps getting better.

App Launch | March 2026

My First App on the App Store

Launching Stamped!—a city passport app on the App Store was a milestone in my developer journey. Stamped! lets users explore cities, check in at landmarks, and collect digital stamps for their travel adventures. From initial concept to shipping, I learned how to design engaging progression loops, polish UI/UX for accessibility, and navigate Apple's review process. Responding to real user feedback helped me iterate quickly and improve the experience. Seeing Stamped! live and available to travelers worldwide was both exciting and humbling—and it inspired me to keep building and improving. If you're working toward your first launch, stay curious, keep learning, and celebrate every step!
